# Shopify Post Purchase Survey Apps vs Knoy Alternatives: Best Options for Small Stores (2026)

You just made a sale. Now what? Most store owners cross their fingers and hope the customer comes back. But what if you could ask them exactly why they bought, what almost stopped them, and how they found you?

Post-purchase surveys give you that data. They pop up right after checkout, when buyers are most honest and engaged. Knoy is one option, but it is not the only one. And for small stores, it might not be the best fit.

This guide compares Knoy to 7 alternatives. We cover pricing, features, and which apps work best for different store sizes and goals.

## Why Post-Purchase Surveys Matter for Small Stores

Before we dive into the tools, let's talk about why this matters. Most small store owners track traffic and conversion rate. But they miss the "why" behind purchases.

Post-purchase surveys answer three critical questions:

1. **Attribution**: Which channel actually drove the sale (not just the last click)
2. **Objections**: What almost stopped the buyer from completing checkout
3. **Product-market fit**: Why they chose you over competitors

This data is gold for small stores. You can:
- Double down on what works (stop wasting ad spend)
- Fix checkout friction (reduce abandoned carts)
- Refine your messaging (speak to real buyer motivations)

### The Knoy Baseline

Knoy is a popular post-purchase survey app. It offers:
- One-click survey setup
- Shopify checkout integration
- Basic attribution tracking
- Starting at $19/month

But Knoy has limitations for small stores:
- Limited customization (one-size-fits-all questions)
- No advanced segmentation (can't target by product or customer type)
- Pricing jumps quickly as order volume grows
- Minimal integration with email or CRM tools

If you need more flexibility or better pricing, consider these alternatives.

## Implementation Checklist: Setting Up Post-Purchase Surveys

### Step 1: Install the App

Most apps install via Shopify App Store. Click "Add app" and grant permissions.

Time: 5 minutes

### Step 2: Configure Survey Questions

Start with these 3 core questions:

1. **Attribution**: "How did you hear about us?" (Multiple choice: Google, Instagram, Facebook, Friend, Other)
2. **Purchase reason**: "What almost stopped you from buying?" (Open text)
3. **Satisfaction**: "How satisfied are you with your purchase?" (1-5 scale)

Add custom questions based on your goals:
- Product-specific: "Which product are you most excited about?"
- Objection handling: "What was your biggest concern before buying?"
- Referral potential: "How likely are you to recommend us to a friend?"

Time: 15-30 minutes

### Step 3: Design the Survey Popup

Match your store branding:
- Use brand colors
- Add logo
- Keep it mobile-optimized (test on phone)

Most apps offer templates. Customize to match your theme.

Time: 10-20 minutes

### Step 4: Set Trigger Rules

Configure when the survey appears:
- Show on thank-you page (after checkout complete)
- Delay 2-3 seconds (don't interrupt order confirmation)
- Target first-time buyers only (or all customers)

Time: 5 minutes

### Step 5: Connect to Analytics

Most apps integrate with:
- Google Analytics (track survey completions as goals)
- Klaviyo (segment customers by survey responses)
- Google Sheets (export responses for analysis)

Set up at least one integration for data access.

Time: 10-15 minutes

### Step 6: Test Before Going Live

Place a test order on your store:
- Complete checkout
- Verify survey appears on thank-you page
- Submit test response
- Check that data appears in app dashboard

Time: 10 minutes

**Total setup time**: 1-2 hours for most stores.

## What to Do with Survey Data

Collecting data is step one. Acting on it is where the value comes from.

### Attribution Data: Reallocate Ad Budget

If 60% of buyers say "Instagram" but you're spending 80% on Google, shift budget.

Action: Review attribution monthly, reallocate 10-20% of budget to top channels.

### Objection Data: Fix Checkout Friction

If buyers say "I was worried about shipping costs," add free shipping threshold banner.

If they say "I wasn't sure about sizing," add size guide popup on product pages.

Action: Review objection responses weekly, implement 1-2 fixes per month.

### Satisfaction Data: Identify Product Issues

Low satisfaction scores on specific products signal quality or description problems.

Action: Flag products with average satisfaction below 4/5, review listings and quality.

### Referral Data: Launch Referral Program

High NPS scores (9-10) indicate customers likely to refer others.

Action: Target promoters with referral program invites, offer incentive for referrals.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Asking Too Many Questions

Keep surveys to 3-5 questions max. Completion rates drop sharply after 5 questions.

Fix: Prioritize must-have questions, remove nice-to-have.

### 2. Showing Survey Too Early

Don't interrupt the order confirmation. Buyers want to see their order details first.

Fix: Add 2-3 second delay, or show survey below order confirmation.

### 3. Not Acting on Data

Collecting data without action wastes the tool. Review responses weekly.

Fix: Schedule 30-minute weekly review, implement 1-2 changes monthly.

### 4. Using Generic Questions

"How was your experience" is too vague. Ask specific questions about products, shipping, or discovery.

Fix: Customize questions to your store's unique value proposition.

### 5. Ignoring Mobile Experience

70%+ of Shopify traffic is mobile. If surveys don't work on phones, you lose data.

Fix: Test survey on mobile before launching, use mobile-optimized apps.
